TITLE: Claim Submission Process

Overview 

The claim submission process is a structured workflow through which policyholders request reimbursement or settlement of medical expenses incurred during treatment. The purpose of this process is to ensure that eligible medical expenses are reviewed, validated, and settled according to the terms and conditions defined in the insurance policy. A well-defined claim process helps improve customer satisfaction, reduce claim disputes, and ensure timely payment of approved claims. 

Insurance claims can arise from planned medical procedures, emergency hospitalization, day-care treatments, diagnostic procedures, and other healthcare services covered under the policy. Customers should be educated about claim requirements, policy conditions, and documentation expectations before treatment whenever possible. 

 

Types of Claims 

Cashless Claims 

A cashless claim is a facility provided to policyholders receiving treatment at an approved network hospital. Under this arrangement, the customer is not required to pay covered medical expenses upfront. Instead, the hospital coordinates directly with the insurance provider or Third-Party Administrator (TPA) for settlement of approved costs. 

The cashless process provides significant convenience to customers during hospitalization and reduces the financial burden associated with large medical expenses. The hospital submits a pre-authorization request containing treatment details, estimated expenses, diagnosis information, and supporting medical documents. Once approved, eligible expenses are settled directly between the insurer and the hospital. 

Reimbursement Claims 

A reimbursement claim is submitted when the customer pays for treatment directly and later seeks reimbursement from the insurer. This commonly occurs when treatment is received at a non-network hospital or when cashless authorization could not be obtained before treatment. 

Under this process, customers are responsible for retaining and submitting all necessary documentation, including original invoices, prescriptions, discharge summaries, and payment receipts. The insurer reviews the submitted documents and reimburses approved expenses after claim assessment. 

 

Claim Eligibility Verification 

Before any claim is processed, multiple eligibility checks are performed to ensure compliance with policy terms. 

Policy Validation 

The claims team verifies that the policy was active on the date of treatment. This includes confirming premium payment status, policy renewal history, coverage period validity, and the insured member's enrollment details. 

Member Verification 

The identity of the insured individual is validated through policy records. Verification includes member name, relationship to the primary insured person, age, date of birth, and coverage category. 

Coverage Verification 

Treatment details are compared against the benefits covered under the policy. This step helps determine whether the requested treatment, procedure, consultation, or hospitalization qualifies for reimbursement or settlement.

Claim Assessment Process 

Once documentation is received, the claim enters the assessment phase. 

Administrative Review 

The claims team validates the submitted documents for completeness and accuracy. Administrative checks ensure that policy details, member information, dates of treatment, and claim amounts are correctly recorded. 

Medical Review 

Medical professionals may evaluate diagnoses, treatment plans, procedures, and investigations to determine whether they are medically necessary and covered under the policy. In some situations, clarification may be sought directly from the treating hospital or physician. 

Financial Assessment 

The claimed amount is compared against policy limits, deductibles, co-payment requirements, and benefit caps. Any non-covered expenses are identified during this stage. 

 

Claim Status Definitions 

Customers frequently contact support teams requesting updates on claim progress. Understanding status definitions helps agents provide accurate responses. 

Registered 

The claim has been successfully received and assigned a claim reference number. 

Documentation Review 

The claim is undergoing document validation and completeness checks. 

Pending Information 

Additional medical or administrative information is required before processing can continue. 

Under Assessment 

Medical and financial reviews are currently being performed. 

Approved 

The claim has satisfied all requirements and payment approval has been granted. 

Rejected 

The claim cannot be approved due to policy exclusions, eligibility issues, or insufficient documentation. 

Settled 

Payment has been completed and the claim has been formally closed. 

 

Common Reasons for Claim Delays 

Customers often experience delays due to avoidable issues. Agents should proactively explain these situations when necessary. 

Examples include: 

Missing discharge summary 

Missing hospital invoices 

Incorrect bank account information 

Illegible medical records 

Policy verification issues 

Additional medical opinion requirements 

Hospital clarification requests 

Fraud investigation procedures 

Providing clear explanations regarding delays helps reduce customer frustration and unnecessary escalations. 

 

Customer Service Best Practices 

Agents should adopt a customer-focused approach when discussing claims. 

Do 

Explain claim status clearly. 

Use simple language. 

Verify customer details before sharing claim information. 

Provide realistic timelines. 

Document all customer interactions. 

Avoid 

Making promises regarding approval outcomes. 

Estimating settlement amounts before assessment. 

Criticizing medical providers. 

Sharing unverified information. 

 

Frequently Asked Questions 

How do I submit a claim? 

Customers may submit claims through the customer portal, mobile application, email channels, physical submission centers, or authorized service representatives. 

How long does claim processing take? 

Processing timelines may vary depending on documentation completeness, medical complexity, and policy requirements. Most claims are processed within standard service-level commitments once all required information has been received. 

Can I submit documents after claim registration? 

Yes. Additional documentation may be submitted if requested by the claims team during review. 

What should I do if my claim is rejected? 

Customers may request clarification regarding rejection reasons and follow available appeal or grievance procedures where applicable.
